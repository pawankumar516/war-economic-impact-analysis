# war-economic-impact-analysis
Conflict Impact on Economic Indicators – Data Analysis Project
Project Overview

Conflicts and wars have severe economic and social consequences for countries. In addition to human loss, conflicts disrupt economic growth, increase unemployment, damage industries, and push populations into poverty. Understanding these impacts is essential for policymakers, governments, and international organizations to design effective recovery strategies.
This project analyzes a dataset related to global conflicts and their economic consequences. The analysis focuses on how conflicts influence GDP loss, unemployment, poverty levels, regional economic damage, and sector recovery.
Using data analysis and visualizations, the project identifies patterns in economic shocks caused by conflicts and evaluates how different regions and sectors respond and recover.

Business Problem

Conflicts significantly disrupt national economies, but the scale and nature of the economic impact vary across regions, sectors, and conflict durations. Governments and international organizations need data-driven insights to understand:
How conflicts affect economic stability
Which sectors and regions are most vulnerable
How quickly economies recover after conflicts end
This project aims to analyze conflict-related economic data to uncover insights that can help guide post-conflict recovery planning and economic policy decisions.

 Key Business Questions

The analysis focuses on the following questions:

How does the status of a conflict affect a country's GDP?
What is the relationship between GDP loss and poverty?
How does conflict impact youth unemployment compared to overall unemployment?
Which region suffers the most average economic damage from conflicts?
Which economic sectors are the most affected by conflicts?
Does the length of a conflict determine how bad the economic shock will be?
Which regions show the largest unemployment spikes?
Which regions recover faster after conflicts end?
Which sector recovers fastest post-conflict?
Which wars pushed the largest share of population into extreme poverty?

Project Objectives

Analyze economic damage caused by conflicts
Identify regional differences in economic impact
Understand unemployment patterns during conflicts
Evaluate sector-level economic disruption
Examine recovery trends after conflicts end
